Discussion: Without accounting, we lack Trust in trade. We cannot effectively tax, track resources, or invest capital confidently.

A Condensed History: Ancient Origins

Mesopotamia (3000 BCE)

Clay tablets used to track grain, livestock, and taxes. Accounting = Trust in trade.

Ancient Egypt (c. 2000 BCE)

Records of rations for pyramid workers (bread & beer). Enabled large-scale projects.

Roman Accounting

State records of taxes, military spending, and censuses. Accounting became essential for governance.

The Father of Accounting

Luca Pacioli (1494)

Renaissance & Double-Entry Bookkeeping.

Emergence of accurate record-keeping due to Italian trade.

He formalized the system of Debits and Credits we still use today.

Industrial to Digital

Industrial Revolution

1760 - 1840

Growth in commerce led to complex records.

Emergence of Auditing and professional orgs (AICPA).

1980s - Present

Why do they differ?

Revenue

Recorded when earned, even if cash isn't collected yet (Accounts Receivable).

Expense

Recorded when incurred, even if cash isn't paid yet (Accounts Payable).

We need the Cash Flow statement to reconcile Net Income to actual Cash.

Accrual Accounting

Matches revenues and expenses to the period in which they occur, not when cash is exchanged.

Discussion: Why?

Why not just use cash?

Economic Reality

Cash timing can be manipulated or lumpy. Accrual shows true performance.

The expense is recognized when it contributes to generating sales (Matching Principle).

Key Points on Accrual

  • Involves judgment and estimation (bias risk).
  • Provides more relevant information than cash accounting.
  • Better reflects economic reality.
  • Asset/Liability Creation: Timing differences create things like Accounts Receivable, Payable, Prepaid Rent, and Deferred Revenue.

Why Do We Need Auditors?

The Agency Problem: Managers (Agents) run the company, while Investors (Principals) own it.

Incentive

(Agency Cost)

Managers might skew numbers to get bonuses or boost stock price.

Problem

(Information Asymmetry)

Investors can't check every transaction inside the company.

The Solution

The Auditor

An independent referee who provides assurance that financial statements are fair and accurate.

The Auditor Report

Public statements must be audited by an independent firm (e.g., Deloitte, EY, PwC, KPMG).

They express an Opinion on whether the statements follow GAAP.

Types of Opinions

Unqualified

Clean. The statements are accurate. This is the goal.

Qualified

"Except For". Generally accurate, but one area is not GAAP compliant.

Adverse

Fail. Statements are misleading and misstated.

Disclaimer

No Opinion. Auditor could not obtain enough evidence.

Example: General Motors (2008)

Concept: Going Concern

The assumption that a company will continue operating for the foreseeable future (typically 12 months). A "Going Concern Warning" means auditors have substantial doubt about the company's ability to survive.

Auditor Disclosure (Deloitte)

"...the Corporation has not maintained effective internal control over financial reporting... substantial doubt about the Corporation's ability to continue as a going concern."
