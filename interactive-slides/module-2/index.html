Introducing Financial Statements

Detailed Analysis & Articulation

The Balance Sheet

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Assets

Resources owned/controlled by the company expected to provide future economic benefit.

Liabilities

Obligations to non-owners (Creditors, Suppliers) arising from past events.

Equity

Owner financing (Contributed Capital + Retained Earnings).

Asset Recognition Criteria

To be reported on the Balance Sheet, an item must meet two critical conditions:

  1. It must be owned or controlled by the company.
  2. It must arise from a past transaction or event.

Discussion

Are "Employees" assets?

No. You cannot own people. Therefore, human capital is not on the Balance Sheet (despite being valuable).

Polling: Equipment

Company XYZ buys equipment for $50,000. Is this an Asset?

Yes. It is owned, result of a past transaction, and provides future benefit.

Polling: Litigation

Company expects to win a lawsuit settlement. Is the potential settlement an Asset?

No. Gain contingencies are not recorded until realized (Conservatism Principle).

Polling: R&D

Company invests $300M in R&D for a new drug (not yet approved). Is this an Asset?

Generally No. Internal R&D is expensed as incurred because future benefits are uncertain.

Exception: Software Costs (Tesla)

Companies can capitalize software costs under specific rules.

Expense

Preliminary Stage & Post-Implementation.

Capitalize (Asset)

Application Development Stage (Coding, Testing).

Tesla Note: "Costs incurred... during the application development stage [are capitalized] to Property, Plant and Equipment."

Tesla Note: Write-Downs

Inventory is valued at the Lower of Cost or Net Realizable Value (LCNRV).

Cost

What you paid to make it.

Net Realizable Value (NRV)

Estimated selling price minus costs to sell.

Calculation Exercise

Tesla produced a battery pack with a Cost of $5,000. Due to market price cuts, the NRV dropped to $4,200.

Calculate the required Write-Down amount.

$800 Write-Down (Loss).

Since Cost ($5,000) > NRV ($4,200), we must write the inventory down to the lower value.

Journal Entry: Debit Cost of Revenues $800 / Credit Inventory $800.

What is Accumulated Depreciation?

Contra-Asset Account

It represents the total amount of the asset's cost that has been allocated as an expense since the asset was acquired.

Cost - Accum Depr = Book Value

Concept: Unrealized Gains

Because Tesla hasn't sold the Bitcoin yet, the gain is "Unrealized."

Where does it go?

It bypasses Net Income and goes to Other Comprehensive Income (OCI), eventually accumulating in the Equity section as Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income (AOCI).

Measurement: Historical vs. Market

Historical Cost

Original price paid. Reliable. Used for PP&E, Inventory.

Market Value (Fair Value)

Current price to sell. Relevant. Used for Investments/Crypto.

What is the problem with Historical Cost?

Relevance vs. Reliability

It ignores value changes (appreciation). A building bought in 1950 is still on books at 1950 prices, which is Conservative but may not reflect current reality.

Polling: Investor Preference

Which method do Investors generally prefer?

Investors prefer Market Value as it reflects current economic reality.

Liabilities: Past vs. Future

Definition of Liability

Probable future economic sacrifices arising from present obligations to transfer assets or provide services as a result of past transactions or events.

Discussion

Give an example of a "liability" that does NOT arise from a past event but from the future.

Executive Contracts: Signing a 5-year contract to pay a CEO in the future. Since the work hasn't been done yet (no past event), it is NOT a liability yet.

Contingent Liability

(The Opposite Case)

Potential obligation from a Past Event (e.g., Lawsuit filed) but outcome is uncertain.

Rule: Record if Probable & Estimable. Disclose in notes if possible.

Polling: CEO Contract

Company signs 5-year, $200M contract for a new CEO starting next year. Liability?

No. No past event/transaction. Service hasn't been performed yet.

Polling: Unpaid Wages

Employees earned $2M last week but haven't been paid. Liability?

Yes. Accrued Liability. Past event (work done) created obligation.

Polling: Gift Cards

Company sells $100k gift cards, not redeemed. Liability?

Yes. Deferred Revenue. Obligation to provide goods in future.

What is Accumulated OCI?

Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income.

It acts like a "holding tank" in equity for gains/losses that haven't been realized yet (e.g., Foreign currency shifts, Unrealized crypto gains).

Polling: Dividends

Are dividends an Expense, Liability, or Neither?

Neither. Dividends are a distribution of Equity (Retained Earnings), not an expense.

The Income Statement

Revenues - Expenses = Net Income

Reports performance over a Period of Time.

Accrual Principles

Revenue Principle

Recognize revenue when performance obligation is satisfied (Control transferred).

Matching Principle

Record expenses in the same period as the revenues they generate.

Example 3: Prepaid Rent

Dec 1: Pay $12k for 3 months (Dec, Jan, Feb).

Question: How much Rent Expense do we record on Dec 1?

$0. It is an Asset (Prepaid Rent).

Question: How much Rent Expense do we record on Dec 31?

$4,000. (One month used).

Linkages (Articulation)

How the statements connect.

1. Income Statement

Revenues - Expenses = Net Income

2. Statement of Equity

Beg Retained Earnings + Net Income - Dividends = End Retained Earnings

3. Balance Sheet

Assets = Liabilities + End Equity

(Also: End Cash matches Statement of Cash Flows)

Class 2 Summary

  • Balance Sheet: Assets = Liabilities + Equity (Snapshot).
  • Asset Rules: Must be Owned + Past Transaction.
  • Measurement: Historical Cost (Reliable) vs. Fair Value (Relevant).
  • Income Statement: Performance over time (Accrual Basis).
  • Cash Flow: Signs matter! (+ Op, - Inv, - Fin = Mature).
  • Articulation: Net Income -> Retained Earnings -> Balance Sheet.
