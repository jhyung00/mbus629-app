Analyst Insight: The "Capital Cliff"

In financial analysis, a Capital Cliff refers to a scenario where a significant portion of an entity's fixed assets (PPE) are nearing the end of their useful lives simultaneously.

High "% Used Up" metrics (typically >50%) serve as a warning sign. While deferring replacement saves cash in the short term, it creates an accumulated liability. The organization will soon face a steep increase in required capital expenditures (CapEx) to replace aging infrastructure, which can severely strain future liquidity and debt capacity.
