1. The Lemon Problem

Without rules, "Lemons" (frauds) drive out good companies.

üìñ The Theory (Akerlof, 1970)

Step 1: Info Asymmetry. Sellers know quality; Buyers don't. Buyers assume "average" quality.
Step 2: The Death Spiral. Good cars ($10k) & Bad cars ($5k) look same. Buyers offer $7.5k. Good sellers exit.
Step 3: Finance Context. Without GAAP, investors can't tell profitable firms from frauds. They panic.
Step 4: The Failure. Good firms leave market. Bad firms dominate. Capital markets fail.
Step 5: The Fix. Financial Reporting is a trust technology to prove you aren't a lemon.

2. The "Green" Premium

Pop Quiz: Why do investors pay a higher price (P/E ratio) for companies with high ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) scores?

‚úÖ Correct! Investors view ESG as a proxy for Risk Management.
  • E (Environmental): Pollution, Carbon footprint. Risk: Fines, Bans.
  • S (Social): Labor safety, Data privacy. Risk: Strikes, Lawsuits.
  • G (Governance): Corruption, Board ethics. Risk: Fraud, Jail.

The Finance Link: High Risk = High "Cost of Capital" (investors demand a higher return).
If Cost of Capital goes UP, Valuation goes DOWN.
